Mycobacterium abscessus has garnered attention as a model organism for studying antibiotic resistance mechanisms and biofilm formation.Researchers explore its genetic makeup and physiological traits to develop innovative strategies for combating antibiotic-resistant bacteria. Biotechnological tools like genomics and proteomics aid in understanding the bacterium's resistance mechanisms, guiding the development of novel therapies. Moreover, biotech companies may focus on producing effective diagnostic tools for rapid identification of Mycobacterium abscessus infections, enabling prompt treatment interventions. Additionally, biotechnological advancements in vaccine development may offer preventive measures against infections caused by Mycobacterium abscessus, contributing to public health efforts in managing antibiotic-resistant pathogens.
